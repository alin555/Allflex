Deepen The Knowledge

Working remotely has become part of our culture!

Some employees might work remotely during their entire time with the company.

A remote employee doesn’t get to “feel” the workplace and you do not get the chance to personally check their wellbeing, especially on their first few days. Here’s some quick tips to keep in mind when you onboard remote employees.

Remote Onboarding- Quick Tips

Follow the detailed planning of the onboarding plan.

Prepare your team to be available for the new employees, ensuring they feel connected.

Confirm all meetings for their first week are pre-set.

Remember to check on and get feedback from the new employees at least once a day. Make sure their onboarding plan is followed.

Respond quickly, especially when working in different time zones.

Don’t skip the buddy part! Assigning a buddy and providing close team support has great significance to remote employees.

The welcome kit is important. Make sure it is sent to them with the IT equipment.

If possible, arrange an in-person meeting at least once during their onboarding period so that they get to know their teammates in-person. If not possible, make sure routine video team meetings are set with the entire team.
