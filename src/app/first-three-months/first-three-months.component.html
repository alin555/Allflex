Deepen The Knowledge

At the end of the first 3 months with the company, the onboarding journey officially ends.

They are expected to gradually work independently on projects, become accountable for their work and be proactive and involved. Still, their learning journey continues beyond the first 3 months and they receive mentoring and support.

1

Support the employees in completing all items in their role-based training plan, including site visits where needed

2

Conduct a checkpoint meeting with the employees to summarize their first 3 months onboarding journey

x

3rd Month Checkpoint Meeting- Recommended Questions

  • How was their onboarding experience? What’s helped them the most?
  • Do they feel they understand the ‘big picture'?
  • Do they have sufficient knowledge to perform their role?
  • Do they feel they can work independently and are capable of delivering the expected results?
  • Are they clear about their personal KPIs and development goals for the upcoming year?
  • Did they get the required support from you, as their manager?
  • How do they feel about their integration within their team and other teams?
  • Looking ahead, is there anything they feel they need to succeed in their role?
  • What can our company do better?

3

Discuss their KPIs and personal development goals with them

4

Set a meeting with your HR person to review and assess your new employee’s performance

5

Integrate all lessons learned from the onboarding process in order to improve the next one

6

Celebrate the employees’ success with the team and congratulate them for their accomplishments so far

Note - If these 3 months are defined as a probation period, decide about their suitability for the role
