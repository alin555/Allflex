Congratulations! A new team member is about to join your team.

Starting a new job is an exciting beginning for employees, though they may also face uncertainty about their new work environment.  A positive onboarding journey helps to build confidence and contributes to the sense of belonging for our new employees. We have a critical role to play in creating a positive experience for them.

This kit is for you, to provide a framework that will make the onboarding experience effective, enjoyable and valuable. In this kit is a set of guidelines and activities for you to complete during the different phases of the onboarding journey. The kit is linked to a set of supporting tools.

What is Onboarding?

When a new colleague joins our team, we aim to:

  • Provide a warm welcome, while building a sense of purpose and belonging to the organization
  • Facilitate a structured and thoughtful onboarding program that connects our new employee to our culture and our business
  • Support the way our new employee integrates into our social networks and starts to interact independently

What do We Need to Do?

Make them feel at home

Make them professional

Socializing is critical for a good onboarding experience. Our role is to make sure the new employees feel welcomed by their team. This role relates to all of us.
We need to mentor our new employees during the onboarding process; make ourselves available for their questions, explain how to handle different situations or challenges on their journey to become professional sales people.

Why do we need an Onboarding Buddy?

Starting a new job is very exciting!

However, people might also feel uncertain and overwhelmed…

To make it easier on our new employees, we’d like to present them with a Buddy to support them on their journey, along with their manager, the HR person and the team.

The Buddy is the ‘go to person’

For any advice, question or need:

1

Guiding the employee on the informal stuff, providing tips that will help them become part of the team.

2

Answering things that the employee might feel uncomfortable asking someone else.

3

Introducing the employee to people at Allflex and providing support during the onboarding plan.

4

Responsible for evaluating the performance of the new employee during the onboarding process and indicating if there are any issues.

Points to keep in mind

Timing is everything -

‘Minor’ things, like having the personal computer set up on the first day, can add to a great onboarding experience. There is no such thing as too much attention to detail when it comes to our employees.

Encourage the employee to ask questions -

It is the best way to learn, yet not everyone feels comfortable asking questions when they are the new people on their team.

Explain internal terminology -

Every company has its internal jargon. Don’t assume that terms and acronyms that are obvious to you are clear to new employees

Clarify what’s important for you -

Articulate as clearly and as early as possible what you expect to see from the employee, how you would like to work together and what you value.

Know the employee -

The new employees are here to build a lasting relationship. Learn who they are and what is important to them to make this work.

Relate to the employee’s experience -

Experience and career story are a crucial part of who the employees are. The knowledge they bring will benefit the entire team.

Customize the Onboarding process to the employee -

We are all different and unique. Each employee comes with different experience and character. It’s important to adjust the Onboarding process accordingly for better understanding.
