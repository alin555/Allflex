Discover What We Do

During the first month, the new employees are still learning about the global and local organization, as well as their specific role.

This is a great time to build their relationship with the team and ensure they are acquainted with everyone.

During this period, we also expect them to gradually take an active part in their team’s tasks to ease them into their new role.

1

Conduct weekly 1:1 meetings
with your new employees

x

1:1 Weekly Meetings

  • Discuss your expectations - explain to them the quality of deliverables expected from their position as well as the way they are expected to work. Clarity of the company’s standards and communication guides the employees for efficiency and good teamwork.
  • Get to know them, learn what’s important to them, what type of feedback they respond to best, how much guidance they need and what makes them happy.
  • Identify any issues or pain points that might come up while completing their tasks, such as knowledge gaps, required skills and missing tools

2

Get feedback
from them on their progress with their onboarding experience after a month

x

1st Month Checkpoint Meeting - Recommended Questions

  • How do they feel about their onboarding experience so far?
  • Any suggestions for improvement? What’s been going well?
  • Was the support received from their buddy helpful?
  • Did the onboarding prepare them for the day-to-day tasks?
  • Do they have sufficient knowledge to perform their role?
  • If not, do they know where to seek support?
  • How do they feel about their integration within the team?
  • Do they have the right tools, systems and resources?
  • Is there anything else that can support them?
  • Anything they would like to share?

3

Follow up with the Buddy after a month and decide together if their support is still required and in which areas

4

Discuss and set their Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
